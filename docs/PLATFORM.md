This is an informational platform designed so that Amazon and AWS can more effectively communicate their early-years educational and vocational T-level offerings to teenage audiences nationwide (UK).

The platform is tailored to youth preferences of short-form content as a demographic, and gamified progression. The goal is to transform would-be walls of text into smaller, approachable, and more engaging chunks where the users are rewarded for how many chunks they consume.

We call these chunks "Snippets", and they are publically-accessible without an acocunt on the platform for SEO and sharing purposes.

Having an account on the platform allows users to engage with "Albums", which are curated sets of Snippets intended to be used to create curricula for specific T-level or similar offerings at Amazon or AWS. For example, a set of Infos relating to Virtual Private Servers, VLANs, or Virtualisation may form an Album for an AWS T-level.

Furthermore, accounts can save specific Snippets to a personal library, which they can read at their leisure, in case a specific topic really interests them.

The platform also has social features in a LinkedIn-style feed with users each having their own profiles, for UK compliance reasons these are completely disabled for accounts which are younger than 14, with posts becoming visible between 14-16, and users being able to post at 17 onwards.

These social features bring value as it allows AWS mentors to connect with prospective young people early, providing support and opportunities to aid them in future careers. It also allows users to showcase what it is they're learning, anything they've found personally interesting, and can function as a feedback system for potential new content to the platform. Liking works as a KPI for posts, which can help quantify how much audience there is for the topic the post refers to. Saving a post puts the post into the user's personal library. There is no commenting feature.
